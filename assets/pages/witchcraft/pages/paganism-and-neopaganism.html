Paganism and Neopaganism

02nd February 2021 - by Andrieli

What’s Paganism?

Second etymological meaning, paganism came Latin “paganus” and means “camp’s men”, in other words: be of countryside and to honor the nature. Beliefs about loving our ecosystem are present since prehistory.

Different than went purpose by catholic church during the Medium Age, pagan was who have lived IN THE countryside and THE countryside, worshiping nature’s deities (their religion was polytheistic).

The hate for paganism has changed its meaning – who was pagan became a bad people and lucifer’s baby, besides called “heretic” by Christianism. But many people don’t stop following their religious beliefs. In the secret form, rituals to Mom Goddess and Horned God have been continued.

Goddess, God and Nature

In Paganism, the feminine and masculine are together: we are the black and white, the receptive and active, the yes and no, the sun and moon, the positive and negative, the sky and earth... we are nature’s duality!

When we work with nature, we honor ourselves because we are nature. But also, many people honor deities, like the Goddess and God (in my opinion, we also be them). The Goddess is the Mom nature/Moon, who gives birth to live, and the God is the Sun, who to do things grow up.

Currently, I don’t believe in deities – for me, our life’s archetypes are in nature-. But also, I believe that deities are only energetic strengths.

Neopaganism

Neopaganism means “the new paganism”, that is, it’s the continuation of old beliefs, but in the present.

In the secular XX, many movements asked of the return of Mom Nature’s cult (yes, there are some differences between modernity/contemporaneity and the past, buut, there is the same essence.

★ Paganism has originated in knowledge animist (all things have a soul), pantheist (unity between deities and nature), shamanic (to believe in transmutations and nature’s cure), and polytheism (believe in many deities).

★ One of many neopagans religions is WICCA, it’s focused on honor the Goddess, the God, and nature.
